Statistics 101

A clear introduction to data, variation, and the methods used to understand them.

What is Statistics?

Statistics is the mathematical science of collecting, organizing, analyzing, and interpreting data. It helps us understand patterns, make predictions, and make decisions when information is incomplete.

In school mathematics, statistics focuses on:

  • Describing data using measures such as mean, median, mode, and variance.
  • Organizing data in tables or groups.
  • Understanding how data varies and what that variation means.

Why Does Statistics Matter?

Statistics appears everywhere: science, economics, medicine, engineering, and even daily life. Whenever data exists — test scores, temperatures, populations, or measurements — statistics gives us the tools to make sense of it.

Some real-world uses include:

  • Evaluating school performance from exam results.
  • Predicting weather using historical data.
  • Measuring variation in experiments.
  • Making informed decisions based on probabilities.

Two Types of Data

1. Ungrouped Data

Individual values listed exactly as they are. Example: test scores of 5 students — 7, 8, 9, 10, 8.

  • Mean
  • Median
  • Mode
  • Variance & Standard Deviation
  • Range

Example formula:

Mean: \[ \bar{x} = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i \]

2. Grouped Data

Data is organized into intervals called “classes,” each with a frequency. Example: groups of heights, scores, or ages.

  • Mean of Grouped Data
  • Median of Grouped Data
  • Mode of Grouped Data
  • Variance & SD (Grouped)
  • Frequency Table Construction

Example formula:

Mean: \[ \bar{x} = \frac{\sum f_i x_i}{\sum f_i} \]
