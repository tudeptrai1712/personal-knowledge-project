Median of Grouped Data

Finding the middle value when data is grouped in intervals.

What is the Median for Grouped Data?

The median is the middle value in an ordered dataset. For grouped data, we estimate the median by locating the class interval where the median lies and applying a formula to approximate its value.

The Formula

The estimated median is calculated using:

\[ \text{Median} = L + \left( \frac{\frac{N}{2} - F}{f_m} \right) w \]

  • $L$ = Lower boundary of the median class
  • $N$ = Total number of observations ($\sum f$)
  • $F$ = Cumulative frequency before the median class
  • $f_m$ = Frequency of the median class
  • $w$ = Width of the median class

Step-by-Step Example

Consider the following frequency distribution of student scores:

Class Interval Frequency (f) Cumulative Frequency (Fc)
55 - 6333
64 - 7236
73 - 81410
82 - 90515
91 - 99520
  1. Find Median Position: $N/2 = 20/2 = 10$
  2. Identify Median Class: The first class whose cumulative frequency ≥ 10 is 73 - 81.
  3. Assign Values for Formula:
    • $L = 72.5$ (lower boundary of 73 - 81)
    • $N = 20$
    • $F = 6$ (cumulative frequency of previous class)
    • $f_m = 4$ (frequency of median class)
    • $w = 9$ (width of median class)
  4. Apply Formula: \[ \text{Median} = 72.5 + \left( \frac{10 - 6}{4} \right) 9 = 72.5 + (1) \cdot 9 = 81.5 \]

The estimated median score is 81.5.

Why It's an Estimate

We assume the data within the median class is evenly distributed. While not exact, this provides a reliable measure of central tendency for grouped data.
