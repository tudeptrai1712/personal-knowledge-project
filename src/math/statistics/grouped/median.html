Median of Grouped Data

Finding the middle value in intervals.

What is the Median for Grouped Data?

The median is the middle value in an ordered dataset. If you have a list of numbers sorted from smallest to largest, the median is the one exactly in the middle.

For grouped data, where data is presented in class intervals and frequencies, we cannot find the exact median because we don't know the individual values. Instead, we find an estimated median by locating the class where the median falls and then using a formula to pinpoint its approximate position within that class.

The Formula

The formula to calculate the estimated median for grouped data is:

\[ \text{Median} = L + \left( \frac{\frac{N}{2} - F}{f_m} \right) w \]

Where:

  • $L$ = The lower boundary of the median class.
  • $N$ = The total number of observations ($\sum f$).
  • $F$ = The cumulative frequency of the class *before* the median class.
  • $f_m$ = The frequency of the median class itself.
  • $w$ = The width of the median class.

How to Calculate the Median for Grouped Data (Step-by-Step)

Let's use an example to walk through the process.

Suppose you have the following frequency distribution for student test scores:

Class Interval Frequency (f) Cumulative Frequency (F_c)
55 - 6333
64 - 7236
73 - 81410
82 - 90515
91 - 99520

($N = \sum f = 20$)

  1. Find the Median Position ($N/2$):

    Median Position = $N/2 = 20/2 = 10$

    This means the median value is the 10th observation when data is ordered.
  2. Identify the Median Class: Look at the Cumulative Frequency column. The median class is the first class whose cumulative frequency is greater than or equal to the median position (10). In our example, the class 73 - 81 has a cumulative frequency of 10, so this is our median class.
  3. Identify the values for the formula ($L, N, F, f_m, w$):
    • $L$ = Lower boundary of the median class = 73 - 0.5 = 72.5 (Assuming continuous data, subtract 0.5 from the lower limit).
    • $N$ = Total frequency = 20
    • $F$ = Cumulative frequency of the class *before* the median class = 6 (the cumulative frequency of 64 - 72).
    • $f_m$ = Frequency of the median class = 4 (the frequency of 73 - 81).
    • $w$ = Class width = 9 (Upper limit - Lower limit + 1, e.g., 63 - 55 + 1 = 9. Or 72.5 - 63.5 = 9 for boundaries)
  4. Apply the Formula:

    Median = $72.5 + \left( \frac{10 - 6}{4} \right) 9$

    Median = $72.5 + \left( \frac{4}{4} \right) 9$

    Median = $72.5 + (1) 9$

    Median = $72.5 + 9 = 81.5$

So, the estimated median test score for this grouped data is 81.5.

Why "Estimated"?

As with the mean, this median is an estimate. The formula assumes that the data within the median class is evenly distributed. While not exact, it provides a good central tendency measure for grouped data.
