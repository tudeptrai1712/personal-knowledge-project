Variance & Standard Deviation of Grouped Data

Measuring the spread of data in intervals.

What are Variance and Standard Deviation?

While the mean, median, and mode tell us about the "center" of a dataset, variance and standard deviation tell us about its "spread" or "dispersion." They measure how much the data points typically deviate from the mean.

  • Variance ($s^2$): The average of the squared differences from the mean. It gives you an idea of how spread out the data is, but its units are squared, making it hard to interpret directly.
  • Standard Deviation ($s$): The square root of the variance. This brings the measure of spread back to the original units of the data, making it much easier to understand. A larger standard deviation means the data points are more spread out from the mean.

For grouped data, since we don't have individual data points, we estimate these measures of spread using the class marks and frequencies.

The Formulas

The formulas for the (sample) variance and standard deviation of grouped data are:

Variance ($s^2$): \[ s^2 = \frac{\sum f_i (x_i - \bar{x})^2}{\sum f_i - 1} \]

Standard Deviation ($s$): \[ s = \sqrt{s^2} \]

Where:

  • $f_i$ = The frequency of each class.
  • $x_i$ = The class mark (midpoint) of each class interval.
  • $\bar{x}$ = The mean of the grouped data. (You'll need to calculate this first!)
  • $\sum f_i$ = The total number of observations.
  • $- 1$ in the denominator is used for sample variance, providing a better estimate of the population variance. If you were working with a whole population, you'd just use $\sum f_i$. For "101" purposes, sample variance is more commonly encountered.

How to Calculate Variance & Standard Deviation for Grouped Data (Step-by-Step)

Let's use the same student test scores example. We've already calculated the mean ($\bar{x} = 79.7$) from the Mean of Grouped Data page.

Class Interval Frequency (f) Class Mark (x) $f_i x_i$
55 - 63359177
64 - 72368204
73 - 81477308
82 - 90586430
91 - 99595475
Total $\sum f_i = 20$ $\sum f_i x_i = 1594$

From this table, we know $N = \sum f_i = 20$ and the mean $\bar{x} = 1594 / 20 = 79.7$.

  1. Calculate the Mean ($\bar{x}$) of the Grouped Data: As mentioned, $\bar{x} = 79.7$.
  2. Calculate the Deviation from the Mean ($x_i - \bar{x}$) for each class mark:
    • $59 - 79.7 = -20.7$
    • $68 - 79.7 = -11.7$
    • $77 - 79.7 = -2.7$
    • $86 - 79.7 = 6.3$
    • $95 - 79.7 = 15.3$
  3. Square the Deviation ($(x_i - \bar{x})^2$) for each class:
    • $(-20.7)^2 = 428.49$
    • $(-11.7)^2 = 136.89$
    • $(-2.7)^2 = 7.29$
    • $(6.3)^2 = 39.69$
    • $(15.3)^2 = 234.09$
  4. Multiply the Squared Deviation by the Frequency ($f_i (x_i - \bar{x})^2$) for each class:
    • $3 \times 428.49 = 1285.47$
    • $3 \times 136.89 = 410.67$
    • $4 \times 7.29 = 29.16$
    • $5 \times 39.69 = 198.45$
    • $5 \times 234.09 = 1170.45$
  5. Sum the $f_i (x_i - \bar{x})^2$ column ($\sum f_i (x_i - \bar{x})^2$):

    $\sum f_i (x_i - \bar{x})^2 = 1285.47 + 410.67 + 29.16 + 198.45 + 1170.45 = 3094.2$

  6. Apply the Variance Formula:

    $s^2 = \frac{3094.2}{20 - 1} = \frac{3094.2}{19} \approx 162.85$

  7. Calculate the Standard Deviation (Square root of variance):

    $s = \sqrt{162.85} \approx 12.76$

So, the estimated variance of the test scores is approximately 162.85, and the estimated standard deviation is approximately 12.76. This means, on average, test scores deviate from the mean (79.7) by about 12.76 points.
