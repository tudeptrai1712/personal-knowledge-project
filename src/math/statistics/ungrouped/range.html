Range of Data

The simplest measure of spread in a dataset.

What is the Range?

The range of a dataset measures the difference between the largest and smallest values. It gives a quick sense of how spread out the data is.

The formula for range is very simple:

\[ \text{Range} = \text{Maximum value} - \text{Minimum value} \]

Example

Consider the dataset of student test scores:

\[ 6, 7, 8, 9, 7, 10, 6, 9 \]

  1. Find the maximum value:

    Maximum = 10

  2. Find the minimum value:

    Minimum = 6

  3. Subtract minimum from maximum:

    \[ \text{Range} = 10 - 6 = 4 \]

So, the range of this dataset is 4.

Important Notes

  • The range is easy to calculate but only considers the two extreme values.
  • It does not account for how the other data points are distributed.
  • Useful as a quick measure of spread, but pair it with variance or standard deviation for more detailed analysis.
