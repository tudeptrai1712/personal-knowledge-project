Mean of Ungrouped Data

The simplest way to measure the average.

What is the Mean?

The mean (often called the average) is one of the most common and intuitive measures of central tendency. It represents the "typical" value of a dataset.

For ungrouped data—individual numbers listed out—you can compute the mean exactly using a very simple formula.

The Formula

If you have $n$ values: \[ x_1,\; x_2,\; x_3,\; \ldots,\; x_n \]

The mean ($\bar{x}$) is:

\[ \bar{x} = \frac{\sum x_i}{n} \]

Where:

  • $x_i$ = each individual value
  • $n$ = total number of values
  • $\sum x_i$ = the sum of all values

Example

Consider the following set of student quiz scores:

\[ 7,\; 9,\; 10,\; 6,\; 8,\; 7,\; 9,\; 6 \]

  1. Add all the values:

    \[ 7 + 9 + 10 + 6 + 8 + 7 + 9 + 6 = 62 \]

  2. Count how many values there are:

    There are $n = 8$ values.

  3. Apply the formula:

    \[ \bar{x} = \frac{62}{8} = 7.75 \]

So, the mean score for the dataset is 7.75.

Important Notes

  • The mean is sensitive to extreme values (outliers).
  • Works best for numerical data, not categories.
  • All values in the dataset affect the mean.
