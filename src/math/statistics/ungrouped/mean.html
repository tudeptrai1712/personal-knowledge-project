Mean of Ungrouped Data

The simplest way to measure the average.

What is the Mean?

The mean (average) represents the central value of a dataset. For ungrouped data, we calculate it exactly.

The Formula

If you have $n$ values: $x_1, x_2, ..., x_n$, the mean ($\bar{x}$) is:

\[ \bar{x} = \frac{\sum x_i}{n} \]

  • $x_i$ = each value
  • $n$ = number of values
  • $\sum x_i$ = sum of all values

Example

Consider the scores: $7, 9, 10, 6, 8, 7, 9, 6$.

  1. Add all values: $7 + 9 + 10 + 6 + 8 + 7 + 9 + 6 = 62$
  2. Count the values: $n = 8$
  3. Apply formula: $\bar{x} = 62 / 8 = 7.75$

The mean score is 7.75.

Important Notes

  • The mean is sensitive to outliers.
  • Use only for numerical data.
  • All values influence the mean.
