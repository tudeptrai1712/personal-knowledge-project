Integrals 101: The Complete Guide

An AP-level overview of integrals in calculus.

What is an Integral?

The integral is the second fundamental concept of calculus. It can be understood as an indefinite integral (antiderivative) or a definite integral (area under a curve).

Indefinite Integrals and Antiderivatives

An antiderivative $F(x)$ satisfies $F'(x) = f(x)$. The indefinite integral is:

$$ \int f(x) \,dx = F(x) + C $$

Example: $\int 2x \,dx = x^2 + C$.

The Definite Integral

The definite integral of $f(x)$ from $a$ to $b$ is:

$$ \int_{a}^{b} f(x) \,dx $$

This represents the net area under $f(x)$ between $a$ and $b$.

The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

  • Part 1: If $g(x) = \int_a^x f(t) \,dt$, then $g'(x) = f(x)$.
  • Part 2: If $F$ is an antiderivative of $f$, then:

    $$ \int_a^b f(x) \,dx = F(b) - F(a) $$

Basic Integration Rules

  • Reverse Power Rule: $\int x^n \,dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C$ ($n \neq -1$)
  • Constant Rule: $\int k \,dx = kx + C$
  • Sum/Difference Rule: $\int (f(x) \pm g(x)) \,dx = \int f(x) \,dx \pm \int g(x) \,dx$
  • Special Integrals: $\int \frac{1}{x} \,dx = \ln|x| + C$, $\int e^x \,dx = e^x + C$

Technique: u-Substitution

Let $u = g(x)$ to simplify $\int f(g(x)) g'(x) \,dx$ into $\int f(u) \,du$.

Applications of Integration

  • Area between curves
  • Volume of solids of revolution
  • Average value of a function
  • Solving separable differential equations
