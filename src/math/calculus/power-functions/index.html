Understanding Power Functions

A core function type in algebra and calculus.

Definition of a Power Function

A power function is a function of the form:

$$ f(x) = k \cdot x^a $$

where $k$ and $a$ are constants.

Characteristics Based on the Exponent

  • Positive Integer Exponents ($a>0$): Graph passes through origin; even powers are U-shaped, odd powers are snake-shaped.
  • Negative Integer Exponents ($a<0$): Reciprocal functions with vertical asymptote at $x=0$ and horizontal asymptote at $y=0$.
  • Rational Exponents ($a=p/q$): Roots; square roots defined for $x \ge 0$, cube roots for all real $x$.

Derivatives (Power Rule)

For $f(x) = k x^a$, the derivative is:

$$ f'(x) = k a x^{a-1} $$

Integrals (Reverse Power Rule)

For $f(x) = k x^a$, the indefinite integral is:

$$ \int k x^a \, dx = \frac{k}{a+1} x^{a+1} + C \quad (a \neq -1) $$

Special case $a=-1$: $$ \int k/x \, dx = k \ln|x| + C $$

End Behavior

  • $a>0$: $f(x) \to \infty$ or $-\infty$ depending on parity and $k$.
  • $a<0$: $f(x) \to 0$, horizontal asymptote at $y=0$.
