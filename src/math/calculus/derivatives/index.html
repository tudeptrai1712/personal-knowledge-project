Derivatives 101: The Complete Guide

An AP-level overview of derivatives in calculus.

What is a Derivative?

The derivative of a function measures the instantaneous rate of change of the function. Geometrically, the derivative at a point is the slope of the tangent line to the function's graph at that point. It's one of the two fundamental concepts of calculus, alongside the integral.

The Limit Definition of a Derivative

The derivative of a function $f(x)$, denoted as $f'(x)$, is formally defined using limits:

$$ f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h} $$

This limit gives the precise slope of the tangent line at point $x$.

Basic Differentiation Rules

  • Power Rule: $f(x) = x^n \Rightarrow f'(x) = n x^{n-1}$
  • Constant Rule: $f(x) = c \Rightarrow f'(x) = 0$
  • Sum/Difference Rule: $(f \pm g)' = f' \pm g'$

Product, Quotient, and Chain Rules

  • Product Rule: $(f g)' = f'g + fg'$
  • Quotient Rule: $(f/g)' = (f'g - fg')/g^2$
  • Chain Rule: $h(x) = f(g(x)) \Rightarrow h'(x) = f'(g(x)) g'(x)$

Trigonometric Derivatives

  • $\frac{d}{dx}(\sin x) = \cos x$
  • $\frac{d}{dx}(\cos x) = -\sin x$
  • $\frac{d}{dx}(\tan x) = \sec^2 x$

Implicit Differentiation

For equations like $x^2 + y^2 = 1$, differentiate both sides w.r.t $x$ and solve for $dy/dx$ using the chain rule on $y$ terms.

Higher-Order Derivatives

  • Second derivative $f''(x)$: concavity.
  • Third derivative $f'''(x)$: derivative of second derivative, etc.
  • $f''(x) > 0 \Rightarrow$ concave up; $f''(x) < 0 \Rightarrow$ concave down.
