3D Vector Geometry 101

From lines and planes to angles in 3D space.

3D Coordinate Systems and Vectors

Points in 3D space are $(x, y, z)$ and vectors $ ec{v} = \langle x, y, z \rangle$. Magnitude: $|\vec{v}| = \sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}$.

Dot product: $\vec{a}\cdot\vec{b} = a_1b_1 + a_2b_2 + a_3b_3$, and angle: $\theta = \arccos\left(\frac{\vec{a}\cdot\vec{b}}{|\vec{a}||\vec{b}|}\right)$.

Cross product: $\vec{a} \times \vec{b} = \langle a_2b_3 - a_3b_2, a_3b_1 - a_1b_3, a_1b_2 - a_2b_1 \rangle$.

Equations of Lines and Planes

Line: $\vec{r} = \vec{r}_0 + t\vec{v}$, Plane: $a(x-x_0)+b(y-y_0)+c(z-z_0)=0$.

Relationships Between Lines and Planes

  • Intersection: $\vec{v}\cdot\vec{n} \neq 0$, solve parametric equations for $t$.
  • Parallelism: $\vec{v}\cdot\vec{n} = 0$, check if line lies in plane or never intersects.

Angles in 3D

  • Angle between planes: $\theta = \arccos\left(\frac{|\vec{n}_1\cdot\vec{n}_2|}{|\vec{n}_1||\vec{n}_2|}\right)$
  • Angle between line and plane: $\alpha = \arcsin\left(\frac{|\vec{v}\cdot\vec{n}|}{|\vec{v}||\vec{n}|}\right)$
