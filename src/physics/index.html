Grade 12 Physics Formula & Theory Guide

Comprehensive overview aligned with the 25 required lessons and key formulas.

Chapter 1: Thermal Physics

Formulas related to heat, phase changes, work, and energy conservation.

  • Lesson 1-7 Heat, Energy, and Phase Change Calculations
    The quantitative relationships governing thermal interactions, used in Lessons 4, 5, 6, and 7.

    Key Thermal Formulas

    $$ Q = mc(t_2 - t_1) \quad (\text{Heat for Temp Change}) $$ $$ Q = \lambda m \quad (\text{Heat of Fusion}) $$ $$ Q = L m \quad (\text{Heat of Vaporization}) $$

    Heat Balance Principle:

    $$ Q_{absorbed} + Q_{released} = 0 $$

    Parameter Guide:

    • $Q$: Heat quantity (J)
    • $m$: Mass of substance (kg)
    • $c$: Specific heat capacity ($\text{J/kg}\cdot\text{K}$)
    • $t_1, t_2$: Initial and final temperatures ($\text{C}^\circ$)
    • $\lambda$: Specific heat of fusion ($\text{J/kg}$)
    • $L$: Specific heat of vaporization ($\text{J/kg}$)
  • Lesson 2 Internal Energy & First Law of Thermodynamics

    First Law of Thermodynamics & Work

    $$ \Delta U = A + Q $$ $$ A = p \Delta V \quad (\text{Work in constant pressure process}) $$ $$ U = W_k + W_t \quad (\text{Total Internal Energy}) $$

    Parameter Guide:

    • $\Delta U$: Change in internal energy (J)
    • $A$: Work done *on* the system (J) ($A > 0$ if volume decreases)
    • $Q$: Heat absorbed by the system (J) ($Q > 0$ if heat is absorbed)
    • $W_k, W_t$: Kinetic and potential energies of molecules, respectively.
  • Lesson 3 Temperature Scales and Conversion

    Temperature Conversion

    $$ T(\text{K}) = t(\text{C}^\circ) + 273 $$
  • Lesson 7 Heat Engine Efficiency
    $$ H = \frac{|A|}{|Q_1|} = \frac{Q_1 - Q_2}{Q_1} $$

    Parameter Guide:

    • $H$: Efficiency (dimensionless)
    • $|A|$: Net work performed (J)
    • $Q_1$: Heat received from the hot source (J)
    • $Q_2$: Heat released to the cold sink (J)

Chapter 2: Ideal Gas

The core equations linking macroscopic state variables (P, V, T) and microscopic properties ($\bar{E}_k$, $\bar{v}$).

  • Lesson 9-11 Gas Laws & Equation of State

    Isoprocesses and Combined Gas Law

    $$ p_1 V_1 = p_2 V_2 \quad (\text{Boyle's Law, Isothermal, } T = \text{const}) $$ $$ \frac{V_1}{T_1} = \frac{V_2}{T_2} \quad (\text{Charles's Law, Isobaric, } p = \text{const}) $$ $$ \frac{p_1}{T_1} = \frac{p_2}{T_2} \quad (\text{Gay-Lussac's Law, Isochoric, } V = \text{const}) $$

    Ideal Gas Equation of State:

    $$ pV = nRT = \frac{m}{M} R T $$

    Parameter Guide:

    • $p$: Absolute pressure ($\text{Pa}$)
    • $V$: Volume ($\text{m}^3$)
    • $T$: Absolute Temperature ($\text{K}$)
    • $n$: Number of moles ($n = m/M$)
    • $R$: Universal Gas Constant ($\approx 8.31 \text{J/mol}\cdot\text{K}$)
    • $N_A$: Avogadro Constant ($6.02 \times 10^{23} \text{mol}^{-1}$)
  • Lesson 12 Kinetic Theory and Temperature
    The microscopic origin of pressure and temperature.

    Microscopic Gas Formulas

    $$ p = \frac{2}{3} n_0 \bar{E}_k = \frac{1}{3} \rho \bar{v}^2 \quad (\text{Pressure}) $$ $$ \bar{E}_k = \frac{3}{2} kT \quad (\text{Mean Kinetic Energy per molecule}) $$ $$ U = \frac{3}{2} n R T \quad (\text{Total Internal Energy for monatomic ideal gas}) $$ $$ \bar{v} = \sqrt{\frac{3 R T}{M}} \quad (\text{Root Mean Square Speed}) $$

    Parameter Guide:

    • $\bar{E}_k$: Mean Translational Kinetic Energy (J)
    • $k$: Boltzmann Constant ($\approx 1.38 \times 10^{-23} \text{J/K}$)
    • $n_0$: Molecular concentration ($N/V$, molecules/$\text{m}^3$)
    • $\rho$: Density ($\text{kg/m}^3$)
    • $\bar{v}$: RMS Speed ($\text{m/s}$)
    • $M$: Molar Mass ($\text{kg/mol}$)

    Visualize the relationships for Isothermal, Isobaric, and Isochoric processes.

Chapter 3: Magnetism and Induction

Formulas for magnetic forces, fields, and the generation of alternating current.

  • Lesson 15 Ampere's Force and Lorentz Force
    Forces exerted by a magnetic field on current-carrying objects and moving charges.

    Magnetic Forces

    $$ F = I B l \sin \theta \quad (\text{Ampere's Force on a wire}) $$ $$ f = |q| v B \sin \alpha \quad (\text{Lorentz Force on a charge}) $$

    Parameter Guide:

    • $F, f$: Force (N)
    • $I$: Current (A)
    • $B$: Magnetic Induction/Field Strength (T, Tesla)
    • $l$: Length of wire (m)
    • $q$: Electric Charge (C)
    • $v$: Velocity of charge (m/s)
    • $\theta, \alpha$: Angle between the direction of current/velocity and $\vec{B}$.

    Direction Rules (Left and Right Hand)

    1. Right-Hand Grip Rule (Magnetic Field Direction):

    • Use to find the direction of the magnetic field ($\vec{B}$) created by a current ($I$).
    • Curl fingers in the direction of $\vec{B}$ when the thumb points in the direction of $I$ (or velocity $\vec{v}$ of positive charges).

    2. Fleming's Left-Hand Rule (Force Direction):

    • Use to find the direction of the force ($\vec{F}$ or $\vec{f}$) on a current-carrying wire (Ampere's Force) or a moving charge (Lorentz Force).
    • Forefinger points in the direction of the magnetic **F**ield ($\vec{B}$).
    • Center finger points in the direction of the **C**urrent ($I$) or the velocity ($\vec{v}$) of positive charges.
    • Thumb points in the direction of the **Th**rust/Force ($\vec{F}$).
    • Note: For negative charges (like electrons), the force direction is opposite to the thumb.
  • Lesson 16 Magnetic Flux and Faraday's Law

    Flux and Induced EMF

    $$ \Phi = N B S \cos \alpha \quad (\text{Magnetic Flux}) $$ $$ |e| = \left| \frac{\Delta \Phi}{\Delta t} \right| \quad (\text{Faraday's Law of Induction}) $$

    Parameter Guide:

    • $\Phi$: Magnetic Flux (Wb, Weber)
    • $N$: Number of turns
    • $S$: Area of the loop ($\text{m}^2$)
    • $\alpha$: Angle between $\vec{B}$ and the normal vector to the area.
    • $|e|$: Induced Electromotive Force (V)
    • Visual Simulation: Faraday's Law
  • Lesson 17-18 Alternating Current (AC) and Transformers

    AC Values and Transformers

    $$ e = E_0 \cos(\omega t + \phi_0) \quad (\text{Instantaneous EMF}) $$ $$ u = U_0 \cos(\omega t + \phi_u) \quad (\text{Instantaneous Voltage}) $$ $$ I = \frac{I_0}{\sqrt{2}}, \quad U = \frac{U_0}{\sqrt{2}} \quad (\text{RMS/Effective Values}) $$

    Transformer Ratio:

    $$ \frac{U_1}{U_2} = \frac{N_1}{N_2} = \frac{I_2}{I_1} $$

    Parameter Guide:

    • $E_0, U_0, I_0$: Peak values of EMF, Voltage, Current.
    • $E, U, I$: RMS (Effective) values.
    • $N_1, N_2$: Number of turns in Primary (1) and Secondary (2) coils.

Chapter 4: Nuclear Physics

Formulas for mass-energy conversion, binding energy, and radioactive decay rates.

  • Lesson 21-22 Nuclear Structure and Binding Energy

    Structure and Energy

    $$ A = Z + N \quad (\text{Mass Number}) $$ $$ \Delta m = [Z m_p + (A-Z) m_n] - m_X \quad (\text{Mass Defect}) $$ $$ E_{bk} = \Delta m c^2 \quad (\text{Binding Energy}) $$

    Parameter Guide:

    • $A$: Mass number (Total nucleons)
    • $Z$: Proton number
    • $N$: Neutron number ($A-Z$)
    • $\Delta m$: Mass defect (u, atomic mass units or kg)
    • $m_p, m_n, m_X$: Mass of proton, neutron, and nucleus, respectively.
    • $E_{bk}$: Binding Energy (J or MeV)
  • Lesson 23 Radioactivity and Decay Law

    Radioactive Decay Formulas

    $$ N = N_0 e^{-\lambda t} = N_0 \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{t/T} \quad (\text{Remaining Nuclei}) $$ $$ m = m_0 e^{-\lambda t} = m_0 \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{t/T} \quad (\text{Remaining Mass}) $$ $$ \Delta N = N_0 \left(1 - \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{t/T}\right) \quad (\text{Decayed Nuclei}) $$ $$ T = \frac{\ln 2}{\lambda} \quad (\text{Half-life}) $$

    Parameter Guide:

    • $N, m$: Number of nuclei/Mass remaining after time $t$.
    • $N_0, m_0$: Initial number of nuclei/mass.
    • $T$: Half-life (s, years, etc.)
    • $\lambda$: Radioactive Decay Constant ($\text{s}^{-1}$)
